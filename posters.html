  • Can We Fix It?

8 hours left. This was the final ad I had to finish, I needed to get this done A.S.A.P (no Rocky). First I had to do some digging. The client used hand drawn images in all their advertisements, they seemed to have a knack for it. They also didn't seem to mind bright colors, but they preferred pastel tones. With my newly acquired information I got to work, I wanted to match their aesthetic of hand drawn but I didn't want to match it too closely. The final design is meant to resemble building blocks, the colors and shading is meant to resemble crayons. Once I finished the overall shape and look of the words the only thing left was to pick the tones. Yikes. After another 30 minutes It was done, and I have to say im quite proud.

  • Light It Up

My only guideline on this project was to include the company's information and their requested quote. While studying the clients website and LinkedIn I noticed their vibe was a modern minimalism approach, and also noticed that black and white was their favorite color scheme. With this in mind I chose to let the typography be the center of the ad, drawing attention to not only the quote but also the ad itself. I didn't want too much color but I did want the name of the nominee to stand out, to do this I chose red due to it's vibrance. Lastly I needed the name of their team to stand out as well, but to avoid color clashing I chose a lighter gray so it would stand out but not too much.

  • What's Quacking

All my life grouchy characters have appealed to me.
Oscar the grouch, Squidward, Mr. Wilson from Dennis The Menace, you name it! There's just something amusing about characters with short fuses to me. So why haven't I designed one yet? With that thought in mind I decided to design my very own grumpy character with an aesthetic and feel that fits my style. The character is meant to resemble grunge style cartoon characters with bright colors and halftones and gradients for the shading. My brother helped me come up with the name and the flame styling on the letters. I spent more hours on this than i'd like to admit, but it was worth it! Because now Grumpy Ducky exists.

  • Next Best Thing

Although I couldn't make my poster for Woodstock, I could still use my design ideas for the poster I was going to make. When I think Woodstock I think 70's summer hippy vibes, it wasn't until doing research that I discovered Woodstock was in '69. Close enough. I went with bright colors and shades that made me think of summer. Bright pink, yellow and orange for the sun, and sky at sunset/sunrise. I also made the supporting text look like things you would see at the beach like a surfboard, and used shades that looked like a popsicle. Ulltimately I was proud of the final product, and this happens to be my mom's favorite of my projects. So it's very special to me.

  • Business Casual

This ad was actually really fun to make despite the simple appearance, even though the design was straightforward the challenge of making the nominee name and team name stand out was still at hand. The company didn't want any color in their ad (except for the color already present in their logo), so it needed to be black, white, and gray far as color spectrum (even though technically black isn't a color). Well if i've learned anything being a designer for so long it's that color isn't actually always needed to make something pop. If you want something to stand out make it differ from the majority. With this learned tip in mind I made the name of the nominee black, as well as the team name and went and made the rest of the words gray. I kept the design elements to a minimum and kept things structured. Mission Complete.
